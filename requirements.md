## 1. Problem Statement

Rural and semi-urban populations in India face major challenges accessing healthcare information and local retail services due to:

- Low digital literacy
- Language barriers
- Limited internet connectivity
- Fragmented platforms for medical guidance and shopping

Existing platforms are urban-focused, text-heavy, and disconnected from local ecosystems.

GramSathi AI aims to bridge this gap using a voice-first AI assistant that works via WhatsApp or a lightweight mobile app.

---

## 2. Objectives

- Provide voice-based healthcare guidance (non-diagnostic)
- Enable discovery of nearby clinics and pharmacies
- Support assisted ordering from local shops
- Work in regional Indian languages
- Operate reliably in low-bandwidth environments

---

## 3. Target Users

- Rural citizens
- Semi-urban residents
- Local shop owners
- Primary healthcare workers (ASHA/clinic staff)

---

## 4. Core Features

### User Features
- Voice input in regional language
- Symptom capture and basic health guidance
- Nearby clinic/pharmacy discovery
- Assisted local product ordering
- Audio responses

### Shop Owner Features
- Inventory upload
- Order notifications
- Basic analytics dashboard

---

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements

- Low latency responses
- Privacy-aware data handling
- Scalable serverless backend
- Multi-language support
- Mobile-first design

---

## 6. Success Metrics

- Reduction in healthcare access friction
- Increase in local shop engagement
- User retention rate
- Average response time
